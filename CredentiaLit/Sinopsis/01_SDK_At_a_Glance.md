## What is the CredentiaLit SDK?

The CredentiaLit SDK is a modular, TypeScript-based software development kit that leverages the cutting-edge capabilities of **Lit Protocol**. Its core purpose is to empower developers to easily implement decentralized, cryptographically-secure methods for:

1.  **Issuing Verifiable Credentials (VCs):** Digital proofs of identity, knowledge, achievement, or authorization that are tamper-proof and user-owned.
2.  **Verifying Credentials:** Authenticating the validity and provenance of VCs without relying on central authorities.
3.  **Managing Decentralized Authorization:** Controlling access to resources, features, or data based on verifiable conditions, secured by Lit Protocol's Programmable Key Pairs (PKPs).

## Core Pillars of the CredentiaLit SDK

The SDK is built on a foundation designed for unparalleled security, flexibility, and interoperability.

### Pillar 1: Lit Protocol Integration (The Cryptographic Anchor)
*   **Purpose:** Provides the decentralized backbone for secure key management and conditional logic execution.
*   **Key Feature:** Programmable Key Pairs (PKPs) enable self-custodial, recoverable keys that can be controlled by diverse authentication methods (e.g., Web3 wallets, social logins). Access conditions are enforced by the Lit network, not a central server.
*   **Value:** Unlocks true user sovereignty over identity and data access.

### Pillar 2: Verifiable Credential Toolkit (The Immutable Proof)
*   **Purpose:** A comprehensive suite of functions for creating, signing, and verifying digital credentials.
*   **Key Feature:** Generates industry-standard Verifiable Credentials that are cryptographically bound to an issuer and a subject, providing undeniable proof of any claim.
*   **Value:** Eliminates fraud, streamlines validation processes, and empowers users with portable, verifiable achievements.

### Pillar 3: Dynamic Authorization Engine (The Granular Gatekeeper)
*   **Purpose:** Allows developers to define and enforce complex access rules for any digital asset or application feature.
*   **Key Feature:** Conditions can be based on on-chain data (e.g., "holds specific NFT," "has X amount of Y token") or off-chain data (e.g., "has a verified email," "is a member of X Discord server"), all enforced by the decentralized Lit network.
*   **Value:** Enables fine-grained access control that is transparent, auditable, and resistant to central manipulation.
